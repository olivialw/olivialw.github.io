Hey there, I'm Olivia Lawrence-Weilmann, a data nerd, mapping enthusiast, grassroots organizer, researcher, and writer committed to supporting movement organizations addressing the root causes of social injustice and inequities.

I'm the Program Coordinator at Political Research Associates, a social movement research and strategy center that studys the entire spectrum of the U.S. Right to support movements that are building a feminist multiracial democracy. I also run digital security trainings with movement organizers and work one-on-one with folks who want to increase their digital security. I view digital security as a form of harm-reduction, and my trainings and one-on-one work use a trauma-informed approach.

My research interests are broad, but ultimately my heart and my politics lay with movements that are building local power, creating mutual aid structures, and carving out spaces in which worlds beyond capitalism and white supremacy can thrive. I’ve spent significant time and conducted research in Northern California, Western Massachusetts, Boston, Madrid, Chiapas, and Mexico City.
